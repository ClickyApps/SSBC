Cashpoint machine
The nearest cashpoints are situated at Tesco and Morrison’s supermarkets - a short journey in both directions from the main exit of the hospital (A34).

Catering/refreshment facilities (in the hospital)
There is a WRVS coffee shop at the maternity centre. This sells snacks and drinks.

You are welcome to use the hospital canteen which is situated in the main building.

There is also a Costa coffee, a Subway and WHSmiths which sells pre packed sandwiches, drinks and snacks.

Opening times for these facilities are displayed in the family unit sitting room.

A snack trolley also visits the neonatal unit each morning.

Other shops on site/nearby
Tesco and Morrison’s supermarkets - a short journey in both directions from the main exit of the hospital (A34).

Laundry facilities
No laundry facilities on the neonatal unit for parents.

Religious and spiritual support
The hospital chaplaincy provides spiritual, religious and pastoral care and are usually available between 8:30am – 4:30pm. They are available “on call” outside of these times.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)
PALS offer advice and support to families. Leaflets about PALS, hours of service and contact details can be found in the reception area of the neonatal unit.

Interpreting services
Language line: a telephone based interpreting service is available in the hospital.

Access to a midwife
Can be arranged through the neonatal unit.

Visiting information

Parents
Open access to the neonatal unit for parents.

Numbers of visitors
6 named visitors. Only 3 at the cot side at any one time but changing over allowed. Parents can nominate a named significant other to visit either with 1 parent or in place of both parents; this will be recorded in the parents passport and signed by parents at an appropriate time after admission

Siblings
Open access to the neonatal unit for siblings accompanied by parents.

Restrictions
Please do not visit if you have a cold or other infection, check with staff if unsure. Due to infection prevention measures no balloons, cuddly toys and flowers.

Grandparents
With Parents.

Hygiene and infection prevention

  • Use hand sanitiser by the neonatal unit door.
  • Take off outdoor coats and hang on pegs provided in main corridor.
  • Wash hands with soap and water on entering the room.

Family and friends
Able to visit as one of the 6 named visitors.

Parent accommodation
There is a family unit with 6 bedrooms available on the neonatal unit.

Facilities for siblings
Toys are available.

Parent facilities
There is a family area within the neonatal unit.

Tea /coffee is provided free of charge. Parents welcome to bring own food in for the day, a fridge and microwave are provided. To avoid confusion please label any food brought in.

Parent support group
There is a Helping Hands Group for parents and babies following discharge from the neonatal unit. The nurses will provide you with information about this group prior to discharge from the neonatal unit.

Mobile phone use
Clearly signposted where mobile phones may be used in the neonatal unit, if in doubt please ask the nurse looking after your baby.

Bereavement support
A follow up appointment will be made with the neonatal consultant.

Dove counselling service contact details will be given.

Telephone and computer access
There is a designated telephone for parents to use on the neonatal unit.

Counselling
Dove counselling service contact details can be given.

Breastfeeding facilities and support
A Neonatal breastfeeding coordinator is available on the neonatal unit.

Play specialists
A Play specialist is available for babies who are on the neonatal unit for a long time.

How to access the Unit
The Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU) is based on the second floor of the Maternity Centre.

The neonatal unit has a secure access system which will be explained to parents when they first come to the neonatal unit.

Talking to the medical team
Parents are welcome to be present at the planning and assessment ward round for their baby.

Consultant appointments available.

Weekly meetings with consultant and senior nurse away from unit are offered to parents.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
You can talk to the nurse looking after your baby and the doctors on duty about your baby’s care.

Ward round
There is a daily ward round starting at 9:30.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
Please discuss any concerns with either; the Nurse caring for baby, the nurse in charge, the neonatal unit manager, the Consultant or the hospital PALS service.

Baby rest time
Regular quiet time is observed in all clinical areas. The nurse looking after your baby will explain this to you.
