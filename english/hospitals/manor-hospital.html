Visiting information

Parents
Open access, Parents can come to the neonatal unit at anytime.

Numbers of visitors
Only 2 visitors are allowed in with both parents at any one time.

Siblings
Baby’s siblings can visit anytime accompanied by parents.

Restrictions
Strict hand washing for all visitors and removal of outside coats No other children are allowed in unless they are over 12 years old. No one to visit if they are feeling unwell.

If you have a relative or friend that works within the care setting. They must not visit in uniform

No one to touch baby except parents.

2 visitors at a time.

Grandparents
Grandparents can visit within visiting hours 2-3pm & 6-7pm, must be accompanied by parents.

Hygiene and infection prevention

  • Wash and gel hands when entering and leaving the unit.
  • Remove your outdoor coats on entry to the neonatal unit.
  • No balloons and cuddly toys.
  • Muslin rabbits are allowed inside incubators but must be washed at home every 2 days please.

Family and FriendsFamily and friends can visit at weekends only during the following times 2pm-3pm and 6pm-7pm and must be accompanied by parents.

Ward Information

Neonatal Units Website »

Parent accommodation
2 Flats available. Single and double room. Kitchen for parents with kettle, microwave and fridge.

Seating/waiting area with parent information leaflets.

Facilities for siblings
There is a children’s wooden play train located opposite the league of friends shop, where children can play. Parents will need to supervise their own children.

Parent facilities
Parent flats. Tea and coffee provided, there is also a fridge and microwave for parents to use. Leaflets and information available. Private bathroom for parents who are staying in the flats. TV and DVD available.

Parent support group
Meetings arranged every month. Bliss volunteers come Wednesdays. Breast buddy’s available to help with breast feeding support.

Parent craft teaching is available on the neonatal unit.

Resuscitation training is available on the neonatal unit.

Mobile phone use
Mobile Phones are not allowed on the Ward, please switch off when entering. Please only use mobile phones in the parent flats or once left the ward.

Bereavement support
We have 2 nurses who are our bereavement team based on the unit. Sister Hayley Amos and staff nurse Jo Timmins.

A memory box is provided, which contains; handprint, footprint, lock of hair and photographs in order to help the parents.

The parents are invited back for a follow up appointment where they can ask questions.

Leaflets are provided to offer counselling by the child bereavement charity and sands.

A referral can also be made to a chaplain or Acorns.

Telephone and computer access
Not available for parents on the neonatal unit.

Counselling
Follow up appointments can be made with the paediatricians.

Referrals can be made for counselling.

Breastfeeding facilities and support
On the neonatal unit there is a feeding room with a static breast pump.

There is access to prompt scheme and a specialist infant feeding midwife.

A team of breast feeding peer supporters, Known as Breast buddies, administer the prompt learning scheme. They teach hand milk expression, and conduct milk expression assessments based on the baby friendly templates and provide support when you are at home. The breast feeding team also does a home visit.

The number to contact the breast feeding team directly is 01922 656822

Play specialists
None available on neonatal unit however if the baby is long term, play specialists from the paediatric ward will be contacted for advice.

How to access the Unit
Ward 28 is on the First floor. Route 127. Press the buzzer to enter.

Talking to the medical team
Attend the ward round where you will be able to talk to the doctors and nurses looking after your baby. If you cannot make the ward rounds an appointment can be made for you to see a Consultant.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
The nurse who’s looking after the baby.

Ward round
Approximately 9.30 every day.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
If you have any concerns you can speak to the nurse who is caring for your baby, the nurse in charge, the neonatal unit manager or a Consultant.

Baby rest time
Lights are always dimmed in the neonatal unit.

Care is co-ordinated so that baby has as much rest time as possible. This will limit calories used by baby to maximise growth.
