Cashpoint machine
There is a cashpoint machine available at the front entrance of the main hospital.

Religious and spiritual support
There is a hospital chaplain available 24 hours a day if required.

Catering/refreshment facilities (in the hospital)
Toast and cereal, tea, coffee and a water chiller are provided on the Neonatal Unit for parents.

Within the Women and Children’s building there is a Coffee Shop serving hot and cold food, which is open 8am – 9pm every day.

There is also a canteen in the main hospital serving hot cooked meals 3 times per day.

There is also a League of Friends shop available for newspapers, books, drinks and snacks.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)
We always try to resolve any issues with families directly, however there is a hospital PALS available, Monday to Friday. Leaflets and information is available in the foyer of the Neonatal Unit, or we are happy to provide contact details.

Other shops on site/nearby
We have a 24 hour Tesco approximately 3 miles away and also a Co-op and Morrison Store with petrol station less than a mile away.There is also a variety of other shops, banks and pharmacy in Wellington which is approx. 15 minute walk from the hospital.

Interpreting services
We are able to access interpreting services if required.

Access to a midwife
If you have been discharged but require midwifery support, please discuss with the nurse caring for your baby for advice on how to access support.

Laundry facilities
We do not provide laundry facilities for parents.

By car

Nearest car park
There is parking directly outside the Women and Children’s Building with designated parking for families.

There is Car registration plate recognition in operation so you pay upon exit from the hospital at pay booths. (View directions here) »

Car parking charges

  • 0 to 30mins – Free
  • 30mins to 2hrs – £2.50
  • 2hrs to 5hrs – £3
  • 5hrs up to 24hrs – £3.50
  • 10-visit ticket – £8 (see below)

Discount Tickets - for regular patients and visitors to the Hospitals
A 10-visit ticket is available to all regular/long-term patients (ie three or more planned appointments or a stay of three or more days). They are also available to visitors of long-term inpatients. Forms to apply for these are available from the neonatal unit, please ask a member of staff.

Nearest bus stop
There is a bus stop in front of the main entrance to the hospital with regular service to Wellington and the Telford town centre and Shrewsbury.

Nearest railway station
Nearest railway station is in Wellington, approx. 15 minute walk away.

Visiting information

Parents
We have open visiting for parents and siblings on the neonatal unit.

Numbers of visitors
We allow 2 visitors per family at one time.

Siblings
Open visiting for siblings accompanied by parents.

There is a foyer area with a selection of toys for siblings.

Restrictions
We do ask anyone who is unwell to refrain from visiting until they are well due to the risk of infection.

Grandparents
Visiting for Grandparents is from 4-7 pm – accompanied by a parent.

Hygiene and infection prevention
All parents are given a leaflet and demonstration on the importance and the steps to effective hand washing.

Family and FriendsWe do not allow any further visitors due to potential risk of introducing infection to the babies.If there are exceptional circumstances where mothers would like a second named person as the supporter, then please speak to the nurse caring for your baby.

Ward Information

Neonatal Units Website »

Parent accommodation
We are able to offer all parents accommodation on the Neonatal Unit for a night or two prior to discharge home.

We realise that going home can be an anxious time, so rooming in with your baby before discharge can help prepare for this.

Facilities for siblings
We have a small selection of toys for siblings in the foyer area.

We have a booklet for siblings, to help them to understand about the Neonatal Unit and the care of their baby brother/sister.

We have baby changing facilities on the Neonatal Unit and a sitting room with dining facilities.

Parent facilities
We have a sitting room/kitchen facility for parents.

Parents may wish to have a break away from the clinical area or have a coffee during morning ward rounds.

Parent support group
We are currently in the process of setting up a formal Parent Support Group.

Mobile phone use
We ask that you do not use mobile phones in the clinical environment.

Bereavement support
We have a Bereavement Midwife, Jan Latham, who is able to offer support to families.

Telephone and computer access
There is a pay phone in the main foyer area.

Counselling
We do not have a dedicated counsellor on the unit but please ask the nurse caring for your baby if you require support.

Breastfeeding facilities and support
We encourage mothers to express their breast milk at the cot side if possible using a mobile expressing unit, however we also have an expressing room if you require somewhere away from the clinical environment.

Play specialists
We do not have access to a play specialist

Clinical Lead
Our consultants cover a week of service and are generally on from Monday to Sunday.So there will be continuity of care for 7 days.

We have a Multi-disciplinary meeting on a Friday where a plan is made for the following week of care.This provides continuity during the following week.

Drs Wendy Tyler (Governance) & Sanjeev Deshpande (Business) are the joint Clinical Leads on the neonatal unit.

Family Centred Care/Developmental Care Lead
We aim to encourage family centred care as much as possible with parents being involved in the care of their baby as much as they are able. Nicola Taylor is the developmental care lead on the neonatal unit.

Nurse Manager
Samantha Davies is the Neonatal Unit Manager, Lynn Atkin is the Nurse Manager

Samantha Davies is the Nurse Manager on the neonatal unit and is available Monday to Friday 8am-4pm.

Other important staff
The community outreach team, Toni Seager and Sian Brookes, provide support for babies and their families when discharged home from the neonatal unit.

Breastfeeding advisor
Sandra Umataliev & Linda Evans, Infant Feeding Advisors are available, who can come to the Neonatal Unit or Post Natal Ward if you are an inpatient to offer support and advice with feeding.

All of our neonatal nursing staff have undertaken breast feeding training to offer support too.

How to access the Unit
There is controlled access to the Neonatal Unit for the safety of all of the babies.

Please ring the intercom at the main door – the staff can see and hear you on the intercom and will allow you to enter.Please ensure you do not allow anyone else to gain access when you are allowed in to the Unit.

Talking to the medical team
Parents are welcome to attend the daily ward round for their baby where you will be able to talk to the medical team about your baby's care.

Near to preparing for your baby to come home, we will discuss the plans with you, so you may make preparation and also provide you with any information before you go home.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
Please feel free to discuss the care of your baby with the nurse allocated to your baby.

Ward round
Ward round usually takes place at 10 -10.30am each day. If you are not able to be there, we understand but please ask your nurse for an update.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
Your baby will be reviewed each day by the medical team, and we encourage parents to be involved in the ward round if they are able.This enables you to keep up to date with your baby’s progress. If you have any concerns please discuss them with the doctor or nurse caring for your baby.

Baby rest time
The neonatal unit has dim lighting and a quiet environment to facilitate the babies to rest.
