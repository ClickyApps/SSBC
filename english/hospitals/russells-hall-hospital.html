Cashpoint machine
The cashpoint machine is located in main reception by WHSmiths. There is no charge to withdraw.

Religious and spiritual support
There is a hospital chaplaincy service which can offer support and spiritual advice to families of any faith. If you wish to talk to the chaplain or you would like your own religious leader to visit please discuss with the nurse caring for your baby.

Catering/refreshment facilities (in the hospital)
The hospital restaurant is situated by main reception and provides hot and cold meals / snacks.

We have tea and coffee available for parents in the flat kitchen on the neonatal unit. For safety reasons we ask that hot drinks and food are not brought onto the neonatal unit.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)
Please speak to the nurse caring for your baby or the lead nurse if you have any concerns about your baby. If you are still concerned you can contact PALS on freephone 0800 073 0510 (Mon to Fri 9 – 5pm).

Alternatively you can email pals@dgh.nhs.uk ».

Interpreting services
We can provide interpreting by telephone or face to face. Please discuss with the nurse caring for your baby.

Access to a midwife
You can contact triage if you are unwell or have not been seen by your community midwife.

Laundry facilities

Visiting information

Parents
Can visit at any time.

Numbers of visitors
2 visitors per baby one of whom must be a parent. At extended visiting times, other family members must wait outside the unit and take turns to visit. If there are any problems about visiting please speak to the nurse in charge or the lead nurse.

Siblings
Siblings can visit with parents at any time.

Grandparents
Can visit any day between 4pm - 7pm.

Please discuss with nurse in charge or lead nurse if you wish to nominate another person in place of a grandparent.

Hygiene and infection prevention
On entering the neonatal unit outdoor coats and hats should be removed.

Any valuables / mobile phone etc. should be kept on your person.

Before you visit your baby, please wash hands at the sink.

If you feel unwell please contact the neonatal unit before visiting.

Family and Friends
There is extended visiting between 2 - 4pm at the weekend for aunties and uncles.

Parent accommodation
We have a flat available for parents preparing for discharge.

Facilities for siblings
We have a selection of books and toys in the sitting room for younger siblings.

Parent facilities
There is a sitting room, kitchen and toilet which can be used by families on the neonatal unit during the daytime. We provide tea and coffee for parents use but we ask that food and hot drinks are not brought onto the neonatal unit for safety reasons.

There is also a bedroom for parents to room in with their baby prior to discharge.

Parent support group
We have a BLISS representative who visits the neonatal unit regularly to offer advice and support. We also have written information about premature babies and the care they require. There are also leaflets which explain specific conditions which may affect your baby.

Mobile phone use
Mobile phones should be kept on silent and only used in the sitting area or outside the neonatal unit. You can use your phone to take photographs of your baby.

Telephone and computer access
The direct line number is 01382 244364. You are welcome to call at any time.

Counselling
Please discuss with the nurse or doctor caring for your baby. You may also want to talk to your Health visitor, GP or contact BLISS for advice. The website for BLISS is www.bliss.org.uk ».

Breastfeeding facilities and support
We have a breastfeeding room on the neonatal unit where mothers can express their milk.

When you are discharged from maternity we can loan you an electric breast pump. This can be used whilst your baby is on the unit or until you are no longer expressing.

The neonatal unit staff are trained to advise and support mothers with breastfeeding.

Play specialists
We do not have play specialists on the neonatal unit.

How to access the Unit
The neonatal unit is on the second floor, East wing. Access to the unit is via intercom.

Talking to the medical team
There is a Consultant round between 09.30 – 11.30 Monday - Friday and he/she will update you about your baby’s condition and progress. If you are unable to attend in the morning please inform the nurse in charge and she can make an appointment for you to see your baby’s consultant. If you would like to speak to the Registrar or the Advanced Neonatal Nurse Practitioner for a medical update please inform the nurse caring for your baby.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
The nurses caring for your baby will update you on your baby’s care.

Ward round
There is a consultant round between 09.30 – 11.30 Monday - Friday.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
Please discuss any concerns with the nurse caring for your baby. If you wish to discuss your concerns further please ask to speak to the Nurse in charge or Lead Nurse in the first instance or the Matron.

Baby rest time
Quiet time is between 4.30 – 6.30pm each day. During this time we play soothing music for the babies.

We also try to co ordinate care so that babies have adequate rest between handling. This helps to promote growth and development.
