Visiting information

Parents
Have 24/7 access to the neonatal unit.

Numbers of visitors
2 per baby, 1 of which must be a parent.

Siblings
Have 24/7 access to the neonatal unit as long as accompanied by parent and does not have diarrhoea & vomiting or a cold.

Restrictions
Visitors must be accompanied by parent and must not have diarrhoea & vomiting or a cold.

Grandparents
Grandparents can visit between 14.30-16.30 and 19.30-20.30 7 days a week.

On Sunday between 14.30-16.30 2 nominated visitors can visit in place of grandparents.

Hygiene and infection prevention

  • Please use hand gel on entry to the neonatal unit.
  • Please wash your hands when entering your baby’s room.
  • Please wash your hands before and after handling your baby.
  • Please do not visit the neonatal unit if you have an infection, please ask the nurse in charge if you are unsure.

Family and friends
To reduce the risk of infection on the neonatal unit visiting is restricted to Parents, Siblings, Grandparents and 2 nominated visitors in place of grandparents

How to access the Unit
The neonatal unit is situated on the first floor of the maternity building.

Please press the intercom button and go to reception where you will be issued with a parent sticker.

If there is no one on reception the intercom will sound in the ward and a member of staff will meet you and show you to your baby.

Talking to the medical team
A member of the medical team is available to speak to 24/7, ask the nurse looking after your baby if you would like to speak to a member of the medical team.

If you want to speak to a Consultant the nurse caring for your baby will arrange this for you.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
Nurse caring for your baby.

Medical team and Consultant.

Nurse Manager

Baby rest time
The incubators are covered to protect your baby from bright light and excessive noise.

Quiet time is carried out during the day, usually between 13.00-14.00.
